# Social Media Content Rules

## Universal Rules (All Channels)

1. **Hook first** — Line 1 must stop the scroll. Never start with "In today's world..." or "Are you looking for..."
2. **One core message** — Each post should have one main idea
3. **CTA always** — Tell the audience exactly what to do next
4. **Keyword naturally** — Weave the keyword into the first 2 lines when possible
5. **No jargon** — Write how a smart friend talks, not a textbook

---

## Hook Formulas (use one to open every post)

| Formula | Example |
|---|---|
| Shocking stat | "73% of people never read past the headline." |
| Bold claim | "You don't need a gym to get fit." |
| Direct question | "What's stopping you from starting today?" |
| Empathy opener | "If you've ever felt overwhelmed by marketing, this is for you." |
| Story hook | "Last year I lost $10,000 on ads. Here's what I learned." |
| Contrarian | "Cold showers won't change your life. But this will." |

## Channel-Specific Rules

### Instagram
- **Length:** 150-300 words (caption)
- **Line breaks:** Use frequent line breaks (every 1-2 sentences) for mobile readability
- **Emojis:** 3-8 emojis, placed at natural pause points
- **Hashtags:** 5-10 hashtags; mix of broad (#fitness) + niche (#morningworkoutroutine)
- **CTA examples:** "Save this for later 🔖", "Tag someone who needs this", "Link in bio"
- **Structure:**
  ```
  [Hook line]

  [2-3 lines of story or value]

  [Key insight or tip]

  [CTA]

  .
  .
  [Hashtags]
  ```

### Facebook
- **Length:** 100-200 words
- **Tone:** Conversational, community-feel
- **Hook:** Ask a question or share a relatable moment
- **Links:** Can include links directly in post (unlike Instagram)
- **CTA examples:** "Comment below with your thoughts", "Share this with a friend", "Click the link"
- **Emojis:** 2-4, optional
- **Hashtags:** 1-3 max (Facebook doesn't amplify hashtags much)

### LinkedIn
- **Length:** 200-400 words
- **Tone:** Professional but human — share genuine insight, not corporate speak
- **Format:** Short paragraphs (1-2 lines), lots of white space
- **Hook:** Lead with a bold insight, a counterintuitive statement, or a personal story
- **No:** Hard selling, excessive self-promotion, cliches ("Excited to announce...")
- **Hashtags:** 3-5, placed at the end
- **CTA examples:** "What do you think?", "I'd love your perspective in the comments", "Follow for more on [topic]"
- **Structure:**
  ```
  [Hook — 1 punchy line]

  [Context or story — 2-3 lines]

  [3-5 key insights or lessons, one per short paragraph]

  [Takeaway / conclusion]

  [CTA]

  #hashtag1 #hashtag2 #hashtag3
  ```

### Twitter / X
- **Length:** 280 characters max (for single tweet); up to 10 tweets in a thread
- **Tone:** Direct, opinionated, clear
- **Hashtags:** 1-2 max
- **No fluff:** Every word must earn its place
- **For threads:** First tweet is the hook; end with "Thread:" or a number like "1/"
- **CTA examples:** "Retweet if you agree", "Reply with your take"

### TikTok Caption
- **Length:** 100-150 characters (short, the video is the content)
- **Purpose:** Support the video, add context or hook
- **Hashtags:** 3-5 trending + niche tags
- **Tone:** Casual, Gen Z-aware

## Hashtag Strategy

| Channel | Count | Mix |
|---|---|---|
| Instagram | 5-10 | 30% broad (1M+ posts), 50% medium (100K-1M), 20% niche (<100K) |
| Facebook | 1-3 | Topic-relevant only |
| LinkedIn | 3-5 | Industry + topic specific |
| Twitter | 1-2 | One trending, one niche |
| TikTok | 3-5 | Mix of #fyp type + specific |

Always research hashtags for the current keyword — do not invent hashtags.
